Caption

Figure 1. Harry Beck’s map of the London nderground from 1933. Beck straightened the lines and more evenly spaced the stops to visually emphasize the sequence of stops along each line.

Alt Text

A vintage London Underground map from 1933, designed by Harry Beck. The map features a schematic layout with various colored lines representing different tube routes. Station names are labeled in black and red text, with interchange stations indicated using larger circles. The famous "UNDERGROUND" roundel logo is displayed in the bottom right corner. The River Thames is depicted as a curved grey shape running through the map. The overall design is characterized by clean, geometric lines, a departure from earlier geographically accurate maps.
